How Can You Help?

Three bills authored by Pa. State Rep.Thomas Murt (R., Montgomery) address Pennsylvania's funding for I/DD services and enjoy broad bipartisan support but have languished in the House Finance Committee since February.

  • HB 625 proposes a 0.5% tax on some insurance-policy premiums in Pennsylvania to fund waivers for developmentally disabled adults waiting for services and supports.
  • HB 626 proposes an additional $0.04 tax on cigarettes sold in Pennsylvania. The tax would be imposed, levied, and collected in the same manner as the existing statewide cigarette tax. The bill would also establish the Adult Intellectual Disabilities and Autism Waiting List Restricted Account.
  • HB 415 would eliminate the inheritance tax that is applied when assets are transferred from a deceased parent to a disabled child if the assets are placed into a special-needs trust established for the child.

To request that these bills be given a hearing, contact State Rep. Bernie O'Neill (R., Bucks), chair of the House Finance Committee, at 717-705-7170 or email boneill@pahousegop.com
